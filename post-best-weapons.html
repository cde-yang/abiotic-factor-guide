All Weapons Ranked: Best Guns and Melee in Abiotic Factor

We tested every weapon so you don't waste resources crafting the bad ones. Here's the definitive tier list.

📅 Updated: November 15, 2025 ⏱ 15 min read
⚔️🔫

Abiotic Factor gives you a surprisingly deep arsenal — but most of it is hidden behind obscure crafting recipes, rare spawns, or dangerous sectors. After over 200 hours of testing across all difficulty levels, here's every weapon ranked by actual combat performance, not just paper stats.

📊 How We Rank Weapons Each weapon is scored on: Damage Per Hit (DPH), Damage Per Second (DPS) over a 30-second window, Resource Cost to craft/acquire, Durability, and Special Utility (armor penetration, stagger, reach, AoE). Tier definitions: S = best-in-slot, must-have. A = excellent, always worth crafting. B = solid but outclassed. C = situational. D = don't bother.

S-Tier Weapons (Must-Have)

🏆 #1: Resonance Rifle

StatValue
Damage85-110 (energy)
DPS~210 (with charge optimization)
RangeLong (hitscan)
Crafting Cost1 Resonance Core + 3 Electronic Parts + 5 Metal Scraps + 1 Lens Assembly
Spawn LocationLaboratory Sector B2 — locked behind a Level 3 Security door. Requires the Researcher's Keycard from the Corrupted Scientist boss

The Resonance Rifle is undisputedly the best weapon in the game. It fires a concentrated beam of energy that ignores 60% of enemy armor, making it effective against every enemy type including late-game armored variants. The charged shot (hold RMB for 2 seconds) deals triple damage and has a small AoE splash, letting you one-shot clusters of Crawlers.

Best mod: Overcharge Capacitor (+25% charge speed, found in the same room as the rifle).

🥈 #2: Heavy-Caliber Revolver

StatValue
Damage65-80 (ballistic)
DPS~160 (limited by 6-round cylinder)
RangeMedium-Long
Crafting Cost1 Revolver Frame + 6 Metal Scraps + 2 Gun Parts + 1 Trigger Assembly
Spawn LocationSecurity Office armory (Office Sector C, top floor) OR 5% drop from Security Guard enemies

The revolver is your workhorse weapon for the mid-to-late game. It deals massive damage per shot, staggers most enemies on hit, and uses .44 rounds which are surprisingly common in the Security Sector. Headshots deal a 2.5× multiplier — a single well-placed shot kills standard Guards and severely wounds Heavy Drones.

The downside: only 6 rounds before a 4-second reload. Pair it with a fast melee weapon for when enemies close the gap.

A-Tier Weapons (Excellent Choices)

#3: Shock Baton

  • Damage: 20-28 (blunt) + 15-20 (electric) = 35-48 total per hit
  • Special: 3-second stun on hit (robotic enemies stunned for 5 seconds). This is the best melee weapon against Security Robots
  • Crafting: 1 Police Baton + 1 Battery Pack + 2 Electronic Parts + 3 Duct Tape
  • Location: Crafting recipe unlocked after analyzing a broken Security Robot (bring the Robot CPU to a Research Terminal)

#4: Compound Bow (Stealth Build)

  • Damage: 40-55 (piercing), 80-110 on headshot, silent
  • Special: Retrievable arrows (80% recovery rate). Kills from stealth do not alert nearby enemies. Arrows can be tipped with crafted poison (+15 DoT over 5 sec)
  • Crafting: 1 Flexible Branch + 2 Fiber + 1 Knife (to craft arrows: 1 Stick + 1 Metal Scrap per 3 arrows)
  • Best for: Solo players who prefer stealth. You can clear entire rooms without triggering combat

#5: Automatic Rifle

  • Damage: 18-24 per shot (ballistic), 12-round burst does ~240 damage
  • DPS: ~280 (highest raw DPS in the game but ammo-hungry)
  • Crafting: 1 Rifle Receiver + 4 Gun Parts + 6 Metal Scraps + 2 Trigger Assembly
  • Location: Found disassembled in 3 parts across the Security Sector. Part 1 in the armory, Part 2 in the Warden's office, Part 3 in the underground firing range
  • ⚠️ Warning: This thing eats 5.56mm rounds like candy. Only use it for boss fights or when you've stockpiled 200+ rounds

C-Tier (Situational — Keep One if You Have Space)

  • Fragmentation Grenade: 120-150 AoE damage. Excellent for clearing tightly packed rooms, but only 2 spawns exist per playthrough (Armory locker + hidden cache in the Basement)
  • Flamethrower (Crude): 8-12 DPS fire damage in a cone. Good for area denial against melee enemies. Terrible against robots (they're fire-resistant)
  • Crossbow: Worse than the Compound Bow in every way except draw speed. Only use if you haven't found the Compound Bow recipe yet

D-Tier (Don't Waste Resources)

  • Rusty Pipe: The literal worst weapon. Breaks after ~20 swings. Only use if you find one on the floor and have literally nothing else
  • Handgun (9mm): 12-16 damage per shot. Outclassed by the Nail Gun which does comparable damage with cheaper ammo
  • Broken Bottle: 8-12 damage, breaks on first hit. A desperation weapon for when you're completely out of options

🟢 Pro Tip: The Weapon Swap Animation Cancel Press the reload key (R) then immediately swap weapons (Q) to cancel the swap animation. This lets you fire your secondary weapon ~0.4 seconds faster than normal swapping. Critical for boss fights where every frame counts.

Weapon Durability and Repair

Every weapon degrades with use. When durability hits 0%, the weapon breaks permanently (cannot be repaired). Repair before it reaches ~20%:

  • Melee weapons: Repair at a Workbench using Duct Tape + the weapon's base material (Metal Scraps for metal weapons, Wood Scraps for wooden ones)
  • Guns: Repair using Gun Cleaning Kits (crafted from 1 Oil + 2 Cloth) at a Workbench
  • Energy weapons: Repair using Electronic Parts at a Research Terminal. More expensive but energy weapons have higher base durability
