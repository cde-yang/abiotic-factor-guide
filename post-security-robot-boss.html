How to Defeat the Security Robot: Complete Boss Strategy

Every variant, every attack pattern, every weakness — mastered. Stop dying to the Patrol Bot and start farming it.

📅 Updated: November 12, 2025 ⏱ 16 min read
🤖💥

The Security Robot is the first real skill check in Abiotic Factor. If you've been coasting through Office Drones and Crawlers with a Makeshift Spear, the Patrol Bot in the Security Sector will humble you fast. There are three variants of Security Robot, each requiring different tactics. Here's how to beat all of them.

🔴 Before You Fight: Mandatory Preparations Do NOT engage any Security Robot variant without: (1) at least 3 Bandages, (2) a weapon dealing 25+ damage per hit (robots have flat damage reduction), (3) the Shock Baton or another electric weapon (robots take +50% electric damage), and (4) full health + full stamina. Going in under-prepared is instant death.

Variant 1: Patrol Bot (First Encounter)

Health450 HP
ArmorLight (15% reduction)
SpeedMedium
LocationSecurity Sector, hallways

Attack Patterns

  1. Baton Swing (melee): The robot raises its right arm, pauses for 0.6 seconds, then swings in a horizontal arc. The tell is the arm raising — dodge backward, not sideways. Side-dodging can still clip you due to the arc width. This attack does 35-45 damage unarmored.
  2. Flashbang Pulse (AoE): When the robot's chest light starts flashing red three times, it's charging a Flashbang. This blinds you for 4 seconds if you're looking at it and within 8 meters. Turn 180 degrees away (look at the floor behind you) to avoid the blind. The pulse itself does 10 damage.
  3. Charge Rush (gap closer): The robot crouches slightly and emits a high-pitched whine for 1 second, then charges forward ~10 meters. Getting hit deals 40 damage and knocks you down. Roll to the side at the last moment — rolling too early means the robot tracks your new position.

Strategy: The Hit-and-Run Pattern

  1. Wait for the robot to use Flashbang Pulse (turn away to avoid blind)
  2. Immediately after the pulse, the robot has a 2.5-second cooldown — this is your attack window
  3. Run in, land 2-3 melee hits or 1 fully charged heavy attack
  4. Back away before it recovers — the robot will almost always follow with a Baton Swing
  5. If it charges, roll to the side and punish the recovery — the Charge Rush has a 2-second recovery animation
  6. Repeat until dead. With a 25+ damage weapon, this takes about 15-20 cycles
🟢 Pro Tip: Use Doorways as Choke Points Patrol Bots cannot fit through standard doorways — they have to crouch to pass through, which takes ~2 seconds. Lure the bot to a doorway, hit it while it transitions, then retreat through the door. The bot has to crouch again to follow you. This trivializes the fight.

Variant 2: Heavy Bot (Mid-Game Wall)

Health850 HP
ArmorHeavy (35% reduction)
SpeedSlow
LocationLaboratory Sector, armory corridors

The Heavy Bot is where most players get stuck. It has almost double the HP and significantly more armor than the Patrol Bot. Physical weapons without armor penetration feel like hitting a wall with a spoon.

New Attack: Shoulder-Mounted Cannon

In addition to all Patrol Bot attacks (with increased damage), the Heavy Bot has a ranged cannon attack. The tell: a red laser sight appears on your character for 1.5 seconds before firing. The cannon shot does 70-90 damage and has minor splash. This attack is the #1 cause of deaths against this boss.

  • Counter: When you see the red laser, immediately sprint perpendicular to the bot. Do not dodge roll — sprinting is faster. The cannon tracks your position at the moment of firing, so changing direction at the last moment avoids it consistently
  • Safe zones: The cannon cannot hit you if you're within 3 meters of the bot (minimum range). If you're already in melee range when the laser appears, stay close and strafe behind the bot

Heavy Bot Strategy

  1. Weapon requirement: Minimum 40+ damage per hit or electric-type damage. The Shock Baton is by far the best weapon here — the electric component bypasses armor entirely
  2. Stay at mid-range (5-10 meters) to bait the cannon. The cannon has a longer recovery than melee attacks — 3.5 seconds
  3. After dodging the cannon, rush in for 3-4 hits then retreat to mid-range again
  4. When the bot drops below 30% HP, it enters "Overdrive Mode" — attack speed increases by 30% and it spams Flashbang more frequently. Play more cautiously here
🔴 Critical: The Overdrive Self-Destruct When the Heavy Bot's HP drops below 5%, its chest light turns solid red and it emits a continuous beep. It will self-destruct in 5 seconds dealing 200+ AoE damage in a 15-meter radius. Kill it fast or RUN. The self-destruct can one-shot you through walls if you're too close.

Variant 3: Mounted Turret (Environmental Boss)

Health300 HP
ArmorFront only (75% reduction from front)
Rotation180 degrees, 60°/sec
LocationSecurity checkpoints, Lab entrances

Mounted Turrets aren't technically bosses, but they're deadlier than most boss encounters if you don't know the trick. They fire continuous 15-damage rounds at 4 shots/second — that's 60 DPS, which kills an unarmored player in under 3 seconds.

Three Ways to Handle Turrets:

  1. Hack It (Best Method): Approach from behind (the turret has a 120-degree blind spot behind it), interact with the access panel, and pass the Hacking minigame (requires Level 2 Hacking skill). A hacked turret fights FOR you for 5 minutes
  2. EMP Grenade (Fastest): An EMP Grenade disables a turret for 30 seconds. Use that window to either destroy it from the front or hack it without needing to sneak behind
  3. Corridor Flanking (No Items Required): Turrets have a 0.8-second lock-on time. Sprint from cover to cover — the turret can't acquire you fast enough if you're only exposed for <0.8 seconds at a time. Shoot it during the brief moments it's tracking you

Farming tip: Patrol Bots in the Security Sector respawn every 20 real-world minutes. A single loop of the three hallways (starting from the Security Office, going east to west) lets you kill all three in about 8 minutes once you've mastered the fight. That's roughly 9 Metal Scraps, 3 CPUs, and a good chance at a Battery Pack every 20 minutes — excellent for mid-game resource accumulation.
