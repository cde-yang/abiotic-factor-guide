How to Survive Your First Night in Abiotic Factor

Don't panic. Here's exactly what to do from the moment you spawn until the lights come back on.

📅 Updated: November 8, 2025 ⏱ 12 min read 👤 By Survivor Team
🌙🏢

Your first 20 minutes in Abiotic Factor are the most dangerous. You have no weapons, no food, no safe room, and the facility's night cycle brings out more aggressive enemies with reduced visibility. This guide walks you through a proven survival route that works every time, whether you're playing solo or co-op.

💡 TL;DR for Speedrunners Grab the crowbar in Office Sector A → collect 3 food cans from the cafeteria → barricade the Storage Room near the tram station → craft a Makeshift Spear → wait out the night. Read below for the detailed version with all locations.

Phase 1: The First 5 Minutes — Loot Everything

You spawn in the Office Sector A break room. Do not rush out into the hallway yet. The first room alone contains enough starter loot to get you through the initial hour.

Step 1: Search Your Spawn Room

  • Check the lockers — you'll find a Lab Coat (+5% radiation resistance, minimal but early-game useful) or a Dirty Vest (small physical defense boost)
  • Open every desk drawer — you're looking for Office Supplies (used for crafting Bandages later), Plastic Scraps, and occasionally a Pocket Knife (the earliest melee weapon)
  • Look behind the vending machine near the break room door — there's a high chance of a single food item (Canned Beans or Protein Bar) that new players frequently miss

Step 2: Secure a Light Source

The facility's lighting is unreliable. When the night cycle hits (roughly 30 minutes into gameplay), unlit corridors become death traps. Grab a light source early:

  • Flashlight (best option): Found on desk surfaces in the Security Office one hallway north of spawn. Turn left at the first intersection, it's the room with the broken surveillance monitor
  • Glow Stick (fallback): Check the Emergency Supply boxes marked with a green cross. Provides dimmer light but doesn't require batteries
  • Craft a Torch (desperation option): Combine 1 Wood Scrap + 1 Cloth Scrap at any crafting menu. Limited duration (~5 min) but better than blindness
🟢 Pro Tip: Battery Management Flashlights burn through batteries in about 15 minutes of continuous use. Turn it OFF when navigating lit areas. Batteries can be scavenged from dead Office Drones and computer terminals in the IT wing.

Phase 2: Minutes 5-10 — Find Food and Water

Your hunger and thirst meters start depleting after about 10 minutes. Dehydration kills faster than starvation — prioritize water.

Phase 3: Minutes 10-15 — Craft Your First Weapon

You can survive the first two enemy types (Office Drones and Crawlers) with stealth alone. But when a Guard-type enemy spots you, running isn't always an option. Here's exactly what to craft:

Option A: Makeshift Spear (Recommended for Beginners)

  • Recipe: 1 Long Pipe + 1 Knife + 1 Duct Tape
  • Long Pipe: Found in the Maintenance Closet near the Generator Room (take the east staircase down one floor)
  • Knife: Cafeteria kitchen drawer OR crafted from 2 Metal Scraps at a Workbench
  • Duct Tape: Extremely common in office desks and supply closets — you should already have 2-3 rolls
  • Damage: 18-24 (piercing). Has reach advantage — you can hit enemies from slightly outside their melee range

Option B: Reinforced Pipe (Higher Damage, Shorter Reach)

  • Recipe: 1 Metal Pipe + 2 Metal Scraps + 1 Duct Tape
  • Damage: 22-30 (blunt). Better against armored enemies but requires getting closer

🟢 Pro Tip: The Crowbar Is a Multi-Tool The crowbar found in Office Sector A (leaning against the wall in the Maintenance Closet) is not just a weapon — it can pry open locked drawers and vents that normal interaction can't access. Always carry one even after you've upgraded your weapon.

Phase 4: Minutes 15-20 — Find and Secure a Safe Room

You need a room with: (1) a door that closes, (2) no enemy spawn points inside, and (3) proximity to a water source. Here are the three best early-game safe rooms ranked:

🥇 S-Tier: Storage Room near Tram Station

  • Location: Take the north elevator from Office Sector A, go left at the Tram Station, it's the second door on the right
  • Pros: Heavy metal door, zero enemy spawns, water cooler in the adjacent Tram Station, crafting bench already inside
  • Cons: Slightly far from food sources. Stock up before nightfall

🥈 A-Tier: Security Office (Office Sector A)

  • Location: One hallway north of your spawn room
  • Pros: Closest safe room to spawn, working terminal for lore/data, locker storage for your items
  • Cons: Glass windows — enemies can see you through them. Crouch when inside at night

🥉 B-Tier: Cafeteria Back Room

  • Location: Behind the serving counter in the Office Sector B cafeteria
  • Pros: Literally surrounded by food. Infinite water from the kitchen sink
  • Cons: Thin door, occasional Crawler spawns in the main cafeteria area. Barricade is mandatory

Phase 5: Surviving Until Dawn — Night Cycle Strategy

The night cycle lasts approximately 12 real-world minutes. During this time:

  • Enemy detection range increases by 40% (they see you from further away)
  • Hostile entity spawn rates double in unlit areas
  • Your character gets the "Uneasy" status effect (-10% stamina regen) if left in complete darkness for more than 2 minutes

What to do during the night:

  1. Barricade the door — use the crafting menu to build a Door Barricade (2 Wood Scraps + 1 Duct Tape). This prevents anything from breaking in
  2. Craft supplies — use the downtime to craft Bandages (1 Cloth + 1 Office Supplies), extra Torches, and repair your weapons
  3. Manage inventory — drop unnecessary items in your safe room. They persist. Focus on carrying: 1 weapon, 1 light source, 2 food items, 2 bandages, 1 water container
  4. Read collected Notes/Logs — many contain door codes and crafting recipe hints that unlock new areas
  5. Do NOT explore — the risk/reward ratio is terrible during night. Wait for dawn
🔴 Critical: Never Use the Elevator at Night The elevator makes a loud mechanical noise that triggers a scripted Guard spawn at the destination floor during night hours. If you absolutely must change floors, use the stairwell instead.

What's Next After Surviving Night 1?

Once the lights come back on, your priorities shift to mid-game preparation:

  • Unlock the Tram Station → Read our Tram Station guide
  • Craft better weapons → Check the Weapons Tier List
  • Find the Laboratory Sector → This is where you'll encounter your first boss-level enemy

The first night is the hardest because you have nothing. Every subsequent night gets easier as you accumulate gear, map knowledge, and safe rooms. Good luck, survivor.
